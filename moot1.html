Based on the updated version of the Maritime Industry Development Plan (MIDP) 2028 as of December 2023, the Philippines is considered as a global center for marine biodiversity. Located within the Coral Triangle, there is a higher concentration of species per unit area in the Philippines than anywhere in neighboring archipelagic territories such as Indonesia and Wallacea . It has 3,212 fish species, 731 of which are considered commercially important. Immerse yourself in the Philippines' remarkable marine habitats, which are home to some of the world's most diverse and flourishing underwater settings.

You'll learn amazing facts about the various fish, insects, and sea mammals that inhabit these seas. Learn about these species' behavior and life cycles, as well as the environments that sustain them, such as coral reefs, seagrass beds, and mangrove forests. Whether you're an avid diver, nature enthusiast, or simply inquisitive about the waters, deep blue wonders provide a plethora of information and resources to help you better understand and appreciate the Philippines' marine wonders.
